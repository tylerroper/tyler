    Heres some of the key things i think goes into a successful project:

    Having a place such as trello to keep all the information you come up with
    and to keep track of things that need doing and that have been done.
    Theres even a trello for this webpage: Trello.

    Planning:
    Planning is coming up with different concepts and coming to a conclusion
    on what concept would be the most efficient and successful outcome of the project
    involving creative thinking and good communication.

    Scoping:
    scoping is the brainstorming phase of the operation
    finding out what work we need doing to make the plan work
    this involves good team communication and creativity.

    Development:
    This is the stage at which the project develops from
    back-end to front-end, securing the project's foundation
    before giving it its appearance creating a very simple version of the project
    making it easy to aquire and organize all the information going on the site.

    Testing:
    This is the testing phase that ensures final product is ready and working,
    various uses-test are carried out to ensure that functions and specifications
    are spot-on before product goes live.

    Maintenance:
    After delivering product, it now goes into maintenance phase thats a continuous phase
    that seeks to improve functionality, fix bugs, addresses stability issues and offers
    support to end-users

    • Risks

  • Rolling out an app initially with all the necessary functionalities is way better
    than releasing an app with features and innovations that end-users might not be open to.
    It is safe to release an app initially, collect and collate feedback and then update as
    clients and end-users dictate

  • Having the right amount of highly engaging people working on a project is way better than
    having a lot of people as this leads to chaos and little decision making. Also,
    it is important to have a main slack that everybody else can report
    to because it is better when instructions are coming from a sole source

  • A good user interface is a must-have for an application. Users most likely engage
    with the apps that are user-friendly and have good user-experience.
    A good interface helps the user to navigate easily through your website.

  • You need good security because everyday hackers are learning new ways to
    get in and out of our systems meaning we have to keep all our security software
    upto date and active with good, trustworthy anti-virus software

    • Legal guidelines and constraints

  • In order for the app to function in the real world, it has got to go through a series
    of compliance tests. Compliance to laws and regulations, privacy terms, financial and
    technological cooperation as well as secure storage of client information. The impact of
    GDPR on digital projects is urging us as developers to seek permission from client before
    accepting their data and to make sure we are completely compliant about what we want to do
    with it, how we choose to store and access it.
